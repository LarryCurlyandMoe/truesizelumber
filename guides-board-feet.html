How to Calculate Board Feet

The complete guide to board feet calculation, with real examples and an easy-to-use calculator.

What is a Board Foot?

A board foot (often abbreviated "bf" or "BF") is the standard unit of measurement for lumber volume. It's defined as a piece of wood that measures exactly 1 inch thick × 12 inches wide × 12 inches long.

Understanding board feet is essential if you're:

  • Ordering lumber for a project
  • Calculating material costs (lumber is priced per board foot)
  • Comparing prices between different suppliers
  • Planning deck, fence, or framing projects

The Board Feet Formula

Calculating board feet is straightforward. Use this formula:

Board Feet = (Thickness in inches × Width in inches × Length in feet) ÷ 12

Important: Use the actual dimensions of the lumber, not the nominal (named) size. A 2×4 is actually 1.5" × 3.5", not 2" × 4".

Step-by-Step Example

Let's calculate the board feet for a 2×4 that's 8 feet long:

Given:
  • Nominal size: 2×4
  • Actual size: 1.5" thick × 3.5" wide
  • Length: 8 feet
Calculation:
  • BF = (1.5 × 3.5 × 8) ÷ 12
  • BF = (42) ÷ 12
  • BF = 3.5 board feet
Result: One 2×4 × 8ft board = 3.5 bf

Real-World Example: Deck Project

Let's say you're building a 12×16 ft deck with 2×6 joists spaced 16 inches apart, and you plan to use 2×6 boards for decking.

Joists: You'll need about 10 joists (one every 16", plus perimeter). At 16 feet each:

  • 10 boards × 14.7 bf per board = 147 bf of joists

Decking: Calculating coverage for the 192 sq ft deck surface using 2×6 boards with 1/8" gaps:

  • Approximately 280 board feet needed

Total: ~427 board feet for your deck project.

Why Actual Dimensions Matter

Many beginners make the mistake of calculating board feet using the nominal (named) dimensions:

  • Wrong: A 2×4 × 8ft = (2 × 4 × 8) ÷ 12 = 5.3 bf
  • Correct: A 2×4 × 8ft = (1.5 × 3.5 × 8) ÷ 12 = 3.5 bf

The difference is significant! Using nominal dimensions overstates the volume by about 30%. Always use actual dimensions for accurate pricing.

Tips for Ordering Lumber

  • Add 10% waste: Plan for mistakes, damage, and cuts. Order 1.1× your calculated board feet.
  • Round up: Lumber is sold in whole pieces, not fractions. Always round your board feet up to the nearest whole number.
  • Confirm with your supplier: Pricing varies widely. Get a quote based on board feet, not piece count.
  • Check the grade: Premium lumber costs more but may be worth it for visible applications.
  • Consider pre-cut lengths: Sometimes buying specific lengths in bulk is cheaper than ordering random lengths.

Quick Reference: Common Mistakes

  • ❌ Using nominal dimensions (2" instead of 1.5")
  • ❌ Forgetting to divide by 12
  • ❌ Mixing feet and inches (always convert length to feet first)
  • ❌ Not accounting for waste
  • ✅ Always verify the actual dimensions with your lumber supplier
